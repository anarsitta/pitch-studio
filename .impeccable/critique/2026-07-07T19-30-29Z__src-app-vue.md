## Overall Impression
The craft here is well above a typical agency landing — the accessibility/motion architecture is better than most funded startups ship. But the page has one self-inflicted wound: at the two moments that must build trust (the Cases "proof" section and the contact block), it presents **admittedly-fake metrics and all-zero contact/legal data**. The single biggest opportunity isn't visual — it's making the proof and the contact details *real*, so the excellent design actually converts.

## What's Working
1. **Accessibility + motion is real engineering, not box-ticking.** One `motion-paused` class freezes every ambient animation (marquee/glow/blink) via `animation-play-state` (`main.css:61`), reduced-motion has genuine static fallbacks in both CSS and JS (count-ups and reveals degrade), focus moves into and back out of the mobile dialog, and the skip link works. This is what "the site is the portfolio" should mean.
2. **A disciplined anti-slop system that executes its own rulebook** — retired eyebrow → `.hl` keyword, single-accent constraint, outlined (not gradient) numerals, no side-stripes/spring easing. The page looks authored, not templated.
3. **Copy that names the customer's job in plain Russian** — rotating hero verbs plus "приводит клиентов, а не пустые показы" / "результат в деньгах, а не в лайках" satisfy the broad-audience clarity principle and the anti-fluff tone in one move.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] Placeholder contact & legal data at the conversion point.**
- *Why it matters:* Phone `+7 (000) 000-00-00`, `href="#"` socials, zeroed ИНН/ОГРН (`ContactForm.vue:46`, `SiteFooter.vue:38,43-44`) fire the "fake company" alarm for the exact distrustful SMB persona the site must convert, at peak intent. Caps trust regardless of design quality.
- *Fix:* Populate real phone/email/verified social URLs/registration numbers before launch; if unavailable, remove the ИНН/ОГРН line and social row rather than show zeros.

**[P1] Cases undercut the "portfolio = proof" thesis.**
- *Why it matters:* Anonymous clients + an explicit "these numbers are illustrative" footnote (`CasesSection.vue:5-8,67`) turn the proof section into its opposite — the emotional valley of the scroll, violating Design Principles 1 and 3.
- *Fix:* Replace with 2–3 real named cases (client + one verifiable metric + logo/screenshot), even if fewer. If real data can't be shown, reframe as "как мы считаем результат" (methodology) and drop the fabricated percentages — an honest process beats fake numbers with a disclaimer.

**[P1] No visibility of location or submission status.**
- *Why it matters:* No scroll-spy active state on the nav; the form flips to success with no pending/loading feedback (`ContactForm.vue:19`) — wired to a real backend the user gets no "sending…" signal and may double-submit. Lowest heuristic score.
- *Fix:* Add an active-link state via IntersectionObserver on section IDs; add a disabled+spinner submit state and a server-error branch beside the two field errors.

**[P2] Services↔WhyUs content redundancy.**
- *Why it matters:* Development, video, and Яндекс+Google appear as both a Service card and a WhyUs advantage, so WhyUs reads as a re-list — scroll length with no added persuasion, nudging toward the "one more card grid" risk.
- *Fix:* Recast WhyUs around differentiators the services list can't state (one team vs. five vendors: response time, single point of contact, no hand-off loss) and cut the feature repeats.

**[P2] Un-tokenized literals & off-scale radii (detector-confirmed, systemic).**
- *Why it matters:* 22 white-alpha hairlines + one `#000` + 13 off-scale radii mean a border-tint or corner-radius change is a manual multi-file edit. Consistent by luck, not by system.
- *Fix:* Add a documented white-alpha/`border-hairline` ramp and fill the radius scale (or snap 2/8/9/11/12/15/16 to the nearest token); point `HeroSection.vue:127` `#000` at `stage-black`.

**[P3] Two interaction rough edges.**
- *Why it matters:* The mobile `role="dialog"` doesn't trap focus (`SiteHeader.vue:67`) and empty-form submit doesn't move focus to the first error — minor keyboard/AT gaps in an otherwise strong a11y build.
- *Fix:* Trap Tab (or `inert` the background) while the menu is open; focus the first invalid input in the failure branch of `onSubmit`.

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (confused first-timer):** rotating H1 word means the headline reads differently every 2.2s — the core "what do they do" is partly obscured by animation; `Я+G`/`360°` stats decode only if you already hold the frame; "Работа с инфополем" is the one service title a newcomer can't map to a need; no active nav state to gauge progress.

**Riley (deliberate stress tester):** empty submit shows two inline errors but **no focus jump to the first invalid field**; contact validation is length-only (`"@t"` fails, `"1111"` passes); tabbing inside the open mobile menu **escapes the dialog** (no focus trap); placeholder phone/ИНН read as "shipped unfinished."

**Casey (distracted, one-handed mobile):** best-served persona — 46px hamburger, 44px socials, `pointer:coarse` padding, `autocomplete="name"/"tel"` for one-handed autofill. Only friction: hero marquee auto-scroll + blinking badge dot compete with reading the H1 on a small screen, and the pause control sits in the marquee corner, not thumb-reachable.

**Time-poor Russian SMB owner who distrusts fluff (project persona):** goes straight to Кейсы for proof and hits anonymous clients + a disclaimer that the metrics are illustrative → reads +312%/+480% as fabricated; looks for a real phone → all zeros; clicks VK/TG/YT → `href="#"` dead links. **Positive:** the anti-pressure tone ("без навязчивых звонков", no countdown timers) is precisely calibrated to this buyer — the problem is missing proof, not tone.
